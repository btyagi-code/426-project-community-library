# 426 Project: Community Library

## Domain Description
Our project is a simulated community library network. Think book availability, holds, and digital lending across a few different branches, all needing to stay in sync. Right now most of this kind of system assumes one server can keep up, but that breaks down fast during predictable busy periods: after school lets out, over the summer reading program, during course registration when professors post required readings, or whenever a popular new release drops and everyone tries to place a hold at once.

If the catalog, holds, or lending service becomes unavailable, patrons may receive incomplete availability information or be unable to place holds and complete checkouts. Because the system represents multiple library branches, availability, loan, and hold data must remain coordinated across services so that two patrons are not promised the same limited resource and branch staff do not act on outdated information.

The people who actually rely on this system aren't just casual browsers. It's students who need a specific book for an assignment, families who can't afford to buy books or subscriptions, older adults using the library for programs and internet access, and people who don't have reliable internet at home and depend on the library for basically all of that. For a lot of these patrons, the library isn't a nice to have, it's the only access point they have.

When the system is slow or down, the effects aren't abstract. A parent might not be able to check if a book is at their branch before driving over. A student might lose their spot in a hold queue for a book due the next day. Staff might end up with inventory that doesn't match reality, telling someone something's available when it's already been checked out somewhere else. That last one is really the core problem we're solving for: keeping availability and hold status accurate and current across branches, even when a lot of people are hitting the system at the same time.
